About this service

Make or change child arrangements with the other parent or guardian. This is also known as contact, access or custody.

Using this service is usually the fastest, cheapest way for you to decide child arrangements. If you can agree your own plan with the other parent, it is just as valid as one that is given to you by a judge during the court process.

Use this service to decide:

  • where your child lives
  • when your child spends time with each parent
  • when and what other types of contact take place (such as phone calls)

This service is useful if:

  • you are separated or divorced from your child’s other parent and have no child arrangement plan in place yet
  • you want to change your existing arrangements with the other parent

Do not use this service if:

  • there’s been any domestic abuse
  • you have concerns about child abuse or neglect
  • the other parent has a drug or alcohol problem that's affecting your children
