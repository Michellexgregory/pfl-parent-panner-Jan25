Using this service

Domestic abuse

Negotiating with the other person involved won't be suitable if you think they pose a threat to your or your children's safety.

Find out more about signs of domestic violence if you're unsure whether you're a victim.

Report domestic violence when it's safe to do so and you think you're a victim.

If you need to access support, Citizen's Advice has a list of organisations which can help you.

Domestic violence and abuse - getting help - Citizen's Advice

Child abuse or neglect

You shouldn't make arrangements with the other parent if you think they:

  • are abusing your children
  • are neglecting your children
  • have a drug or alcohol problem that's affecting your children

Find out more about child abuse and neglect

Report child abuse if you have any concerns and tell your children about Childline so they can report any abuse themselves.

You can also report a concern about a child online to the National Society for the Prevenion of Cruelty to Children (NSPCC).
